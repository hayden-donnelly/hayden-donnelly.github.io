The Village Sacrifice

"Your village has decided to deal with over population by sacrificing you a to the great stone golem who lives nearby. Your goal is to sneak through the dungeon and escape undetected."

The village sacrifice is a 1v1 stealth game, you vs the AI. As this was created for Ludem Dare I decided to go with a simple state machine solution to control the enemy's behaviour. I could've gone with something more maintainable like a behaviour tree but since the AI only required three states an fsm was the perfect level of complexity (and behaviour trees are just fancy state machines anyways).

Made in Unity.

Santa's Delivery Service

"Race to the finish and deliver as many presents as you can!"

Santa's Delivery Service is a physics based racing game. For the vehicle physics I used Unity's built in wheel colliders. They work well when for simulating semi-realistic vehicles but don't give you a ton of control. This makes it hard to customize the feeling of your car/truck. However they worked for what I was trying to accomplish.

Made in Unity.

Space Janitor

"You've been enlisted to clean up an alien planet but they forgot to turn off the defense systems. Vacuum up trash and dodge bullets"

Space Janitor is a pretty simple arcade style game. You'll have to vacuum up bits of trash to raise your score while simultaneously weaving through enemy bullets. Looking back unity was pretty overkill for this project but it works.

Made in Unity.

William's Project

"Fight for survival in William's twisted test. Fight through waves of monsters and sacrifice your health for powerups at the end of each ropund."

William's project was my first project outside of Unity. It's much less ambitious than some of my other games but at the time I was still learning C and figuring out how to develop games with a "code first and no editor" mindset. Overall I'm extremely happy with how it turned out and have now gone on to develop my own 2D engine. unfortunately I never got around to compiling and packing up the game for release but if you want to play it then everything required for compilation is in the git repo below.

Made with C and SDL2

Vehicle Physics

"Raycast supension based car controller"

I wasn't quite happy with how vehicles controlled using unity's built in wheel colliders so I built this. It combines raycast suspension with various force modes to create a highly customizable car controller.
